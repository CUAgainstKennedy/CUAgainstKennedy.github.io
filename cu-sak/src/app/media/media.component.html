This is bigger than just a voting record.

A flawed process from the start.

The University of Colorado regents ignored the voices of the faculty and students that they promised to faithfully represent. Multiple regents have stated that they weren't even aware of his controversial past and problematic job history before voting him as sole finalist.

A current CU Law student and CU Denver alum was nominated by the TriExecs of CU Boulder, the SBA President from the Law School, the Student Body President from CU Denver, the Class President from the Medical School, and the Student Body President of UCCS, to serve on the Presidential Search Committee. However, the student was denied.

The regents instead selected a staff member from the Center for Western Civilization, Thought & Policy, who was getting a part-time MBA. Only after fighting the Regents last September did they take action to appoint a student to the committee. Even then, they appointed a student that was not nominated by student leaders.

The process included over 30 vetted candidates, with six semi-finalists whose names went to the Regents. The search committee sought to rate these semi-finalists, but that request was denied by Regents.

Lack of Meaningful Academic Experience

Kennedy is unqualified for this position and not loyal to any academic institutions.

He does not hold a Ph.D. or have any research experience. He spent 2 years at Johns Hopkins, 4 at George Washington, and less than 3 years at University of North Dakota. Here's his biography.

While at UND, he was already interviewing for another position within 20 months of his appointment. He was denied the position of president of University of Central Florida for similar reasons. He has no connection to Colorado or the CU System.
